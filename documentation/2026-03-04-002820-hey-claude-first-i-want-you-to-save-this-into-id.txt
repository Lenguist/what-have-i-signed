⏺ Saved to market-research.md. Here's the TL;DR:

  ---
  The space is crowded but nobody has won. There are two camps:

  1. Volunteer/nonprofit tools (ToS;DR, PrivacySpy) — trusted but limited
  coverage, manual curation, can't auto-capture anything
  2. AI summarizer extensions (TermsAi, T&See, Termsy, ~10 others) — all do
  on-demand summarization only. None store history. None auto-capture when you
  accept. None let you come back later.

  The core gap your idea fills that nobody else does:
  - Auto-capture at the moment of acceptance (not manual trigger)
  - Persistent history of everything you've ever agreed to
  - Retrospective AI chat ("what did I agree to with Spotify 6 months ago?")
  - Change alerts when companies update terms you accepted

  Validating signals:
  - 56-98% of people never read T&Cs — massive unaddressed problem
  - Guardio (browser security extension, adjacent) hit $100M ARR and raised
  $127M — proves the extension → subscription model works
  - No dominant player despite the obvious need

  Key risks to be aware of:
  - Platform risk (Google Chrome MV3 changes can hobble extensions)
  - Trust is everything — Honey lost 8M users overnight when it broke trust
  - Pure summarization is now a commodity (any LLM can do it) — the moat is the
  captured history + auto-detection
